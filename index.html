Global Spirits: Crisis, Capital & Opportunity

An investor’s guide to where capital and opportunity are moving as the global spirits industry restructures over the next 3–5 years.

The Map Has Changed. Read the New One.

The global spirits industry is not collapsing—it is restructuring. The $340 billion global market is not shrinking; it is reallocating. Capital that was concentrated in American whiskey exports, Scottish distillery expansion, and celebrity brand launches is being released and redirected. The investors who profit will be those who stop waiting for the old map to come back.

Executive Summary

The global spirits industry is in its most significant restructuring since Prohibition. U.S. supplier revenues are down to $37.2 billion from a ~$39.1 billion peak, American whiskey exports collapsed across every major market in 2025, and the producer base is contracting at historic speed on both sides of the Atlantic. The details of the contraction are well documented. This analysis is about what comes next.

This analysis maps where forward-looking capital should deploy.

I. Where to Deploy Capital

Six actionable themes for the 3–5 year horizon. This is the bottom line—everything that follows is the supporting evidence.

HIGH CONVICTION

1. Irish Whiskey Infrastructure

Best risk/reward in spirits. Tariff-free EU/UK access, consumer preference shift, Jameson’s India +52% / China +99% growth vectors. Entry points: emerging distilleries with aged stock and export capability, or mid-tier brands being shed by Pernod Ricard’s restructuring (Knappogue Castle model). Bushmills $89M expansion signals category-level confidence from sophisticated capital.

HIGH CONVICTION

2. Premium Caribbean Rum

Undervalued. Minimal tariff exposure, authentic provenance, 12% annual ultra-premium growth since 2020, whiskey-crossover consumer bridge in place. Tropical aging = faster capital cycles than Scotch. Target: Barbadian, Jamaican, Guatemalan distilleries with aged stock. The Foursquare effect is creating cult demand. Projected $2.82B luxury rum market by 2032.

HIGH CONVICTION

3. RTD Spirits-Based Brands

Only high-growth segment at scale: +16.4% to $3.8B. Cannibalizing entry-price spirits and beer. Capital efficient (brand + contract production). RTD spirits share still <10% of total—significant runway. Key risk is maturation, but High Noon and Surfside prove the model scales. Look for differentiated brands with distribution partnerships.

HIGH CONVICTION

4. India Distribution Infrastructure

India’s whisky market (260M cases in 2024, projected 502M by 2034) is where the next generation of global spirits volume lives. India is already the #1 Scotch export market by volume. A UK-India FTA cutting the 150% import tariff would be a $1.3B catalyst for Scotch alone. Capital deployed into Indian distribution relationships now will compound as the market opens.

HIGH CONVICTION

5. Premium Tequila with Authentic Brands

Don Julio’s rise to #1 U.S. brand by retail value ($2.6B, +28.2% to 4.4M cases) proves premiumization still works where the story is authentic. The celebrity brand glut (75+ tequilas) is creating forced sellers. Target heritage and additive-free brands with real retail velocity. Agave price crash ($0.10/kg from $1.25) means input costs favor buyers. USMCA tariff moat intact.

MEDIUM CONVICTION

6. Distressed U.S. & Scottish Assets

Attractive pricing (estimated 30–60% discount to peak) but requires operational expertise and patience. U.S. craft roll-ups need distribution infrastructure. Scottish mothballed distilleries offer maturing stock + tourism but demand is uncertain. Stoli/Kentucky Owl liquidation is highest-profile opportunity. Best for strategic acquirers or experienced spirits private equity operators, not generalist investors.

II. The Landscape: Where Things Stand

Volume up, revenue down. The market is trading down, not drying up—and the gaps it’s creating are the opportunity.

Major Producer Retrenchment (2024–2025)

The moves that matter. Active U.S. craft distillers fell 25.6% in 12 months (3,069 to 2,282). These are the headline actions shaping the distressed asset pipeline.

United States

  • Stoli Group USA & Kentucky Owl — Chapter 7 liquidation (Jan 2026). $100–500M in assets on the market
  • Jim Beam (Suntory Global Spirits): Full production suspension at Clermont, Kentucky for all of 2026
  • Brown-Forman: 648 layoffs (12% workforce), Louisville cooperage closed. $70–80M annual savings target
  • Diageo: Halted Lebanon, Kentucky distillery; $625M cost-cutting program; CEO departed
  • MGP Ingredients (largest U.S. contract distiller, Lawrenceburg, Indiana): Gross profits fell 68% in 2024; cutting whiskey output 2026. Industry-wide demand bellwether

Scotland & Ireland

  • Diageo: Teaninich Distillery production paused; Roseisle Maltings suspended through June 2026+
  • Speyside Distillery — Permanently ceased distilling May 2025
  • Midleton Distillery (Irish Distillers/Pernod Ricard) — Production paused March 2025; $270M expansion delayed
  • Waterford Whisky (Ireland) — Shut down November 2024
  • Brown-Forman: Glenglassaugh & BenRiach moved to shared production, reducing capacity

The Recovery Signal to Watch

16.1 million barrels aging in Kentucky, 525 million liters of tequila in Mexican inventory, Diageo’s inventory-to-revenue ratio at 43%. The overhang is well documented. What matters for investors is the signal: when Jim Beam, Teaninich, Roseisle, and Midleton restart production, the supply-demand balance has normalized. Until then, the buyer’s market for bulk whiskey and distressed assets remains open.

III. Other Categories Worth Watching

Categories not covered by the six investment themes above but with dynamics investors should understand.

S Scotch Whisky

Port Ellen (Islay) reopened after 40 years and Rosebank completed its first distillation—confirming that ultra-premium Scotch investment continues even as the mass market contracts. Single malt collectibles are appreciating as production pauses reduce future supply. The India story (covered in Theme 4) is primarily a Scotch story: India is now the #1 Scotch export market by volume.

J Japanese Whisky

The only major whisky category where demand consistently exceeds supply. Suntory increased output 32% (2018–2023) but exports grew 140%. Affordable age-stated bottles are unlikely before 2030. The 2021 labeling standards tightened what qualifies as “Japanese whisky,” further reducing compliant supply. Secondary market appreciation creates a collector/investor demand layer. Limited acquisition targets—Suntory and Nikka dominate.

C Cognac & Brandy

Volumes collapsed 41% from 8.5M cases (2021) to ~5M cases. Hennessy holds 62.3% U.S. share and is the only top-five brand growing (+2%). Rémy Cointreau stock is down 40% from peak. China’s anti-dumping duties (27–35%) and the U.S. 15% tariff squeeze the category from both sides. This is a binary trade-policy bet: simultaneous U.S. + China resolution = significant upside. Only for investors with specific conviction.

V Vodka

The post-Russian shelf space reallocation is permanent—Stoli’s Chapter 7 liquidation closes the door. Polish vodka (Belvedere, Chopin) is gaining premium positioning in vacated space. Tito’s declined for the first time ever (−1.5%). The base category is in structural decline from RTD substitution and tequila migration, but vodka-based RTD is still growing. Non-Western markets (India, Nigeria) show gaps where Russian Standard and Beluga are repositioning.

IV. Forward-Looking Indicators & Capital Flow

Where investment money is actually moving. The signals that separate recovery from permanent restructuring.

Where Capital Is Actually Moving

Diageo, Pernod Ricard, and Campari are shedding non-core brands. Mid-tier companies and PE-backed vehicles are buying them. Here’s where the money is going.

$ Major Divestitures Creating Opportunity

Diageo: Sold Cacique rum to La Martiniquaise; transferred Cîroc to Main Street Advisors; sold East African Breweries to Asahi ($2.3B); halted Distill Ventures incubator. Campari: Bought Courvoisier ($1.2B) while shedding Cinzano ($108M), Averna and Zedda Piras ($108M). Pernod Ricard: Sold Campo Viejo, Jacob’s Creek, Knappogue Castle. Targeting $1.1B in cuts by FY2029.

$ Mid-Tier Roll-Up Wave

Uncle Nearest acquired Square One Organic Spirits. Heritage Distilling acquired Thinking Tree Spirits. Redwood Empire Whiskey grew 30% and acquired Savage & Cooke distillery. Next Century Spirits acquiring orphaned brands from Diageo, Beam Suntory, and Pernod Ricard divestitures. Early transactions of what may become significant mid-market consolidation.

$ No/Low ABV Emerging

Growing 10% annually. Non-alcoholic spirits projected $1.2B by 2034 (7.4% CAGR). Diageo acquired Ritual Zero Proof. Rémy Cointreau took stake in JNPR Spirits. AG Barr acquired Strykk. 61 million new global consumers entered no-alcohol segment in 2024 (IWSR). Strategic portfolio insurance more than core growth play.

Recovery vs. Permanent Structural Shift

Seven indicators that will tell you whether this is a cycle or a permanent restructuring. What to watch and what each signal means.

  1. EU-U.S. tariff resolution. SCOTUS struck down IEEPA tariffs (Feb 2026) but Section 122 replaced them within 24 hours. EU retaliatory suspension expired; 50% threat not withdrawn. A permanent zero-for-zero deal is the single most important recovery signal for American whiskey exports.
  2. Canada market access. Federal tariff lifted Sept 2025, but provincial shelf bans are entering their second year. 3,600+ products pulled from LCBO. SCOTUS ruling has not changed provincial response. Consumer sentiment recovery will lag any trade resolution by 12–18 months.
  3. U.S. domestic volume inflection. WSWA SipSource data shows 2025 tracking toward a third consecutive negative year for core spirits depletions. Two consecutive quarters of positive volume growth would signal the domestic floor has been found.
  4. Production restart at idle facilities. Jim Beam, Teaninich, Roseisle, Midleton, George Dickel, and Balcones are all dark through mid-2026 or later. When major producers restart production, supply-demand has normalized. Until then, the buyer’s market continues.
  5. China luxury spirits demand. Scotch exports to China fell 31.5%. Cognac exports to China fell 24% in value. Anti-dumping duties on EU brandy are formalized at 27–35%. There is no clear recovery timeline. A China rebound would lift Scotch and cognac simultaneously.
  6. Craft distillery net openings. The U.S. lost 787 active craft distillers in 12 months (25.6% decline). A return to net positive openings would signal renewed entrepreneurial confidence in the category. Currently accelerating negative.
  7. Gen Z consumption trajectory. This generation is entering legal drinking age with materially lower alcohol consumption rates than Millennials at the same age. If this pattern holds as they age, it represents a 15–25 year structural drag on total spirits volume. The industry’s pivot to no/low ABV and RTD formats is the strategic response.

What This Means for Capital Deployment

IWSR characterizes the current decline as “more cyclical than structural.” The Gen Z data and inventory overhang suggest the recovery, when it comes, will restore a smaller market at lower margin than the 2019–2022 peak. Deploy capital into categories and regions that are structurally gaining—Irish whiskey, premium rum, Japanese whisky, RTD, India—rather than betting on a full return to the pre-2018 American spirits export paradigm.

Capital Traps to Avoid

  • Betting on bourbon export recovery without a durable trade deal: The SCOTUS ruling struck down IEEPA tariffs but the administration replaced them with Section 122 within 24 hours. EU retaliatory suspension expired and the 50% threat remains. Don’t invest in export-dependent American whiskey capacity until a permanent zero-for-zero agreement is in place—not just a pause.
  • Celebrity spirits brand acquisitions: 75+ celebrity tequilas. Most have zero enterprise value independent of their famous founder. Casamigos, Don Julio, and 818 face class-action lawsuits over 100% agave claims. Unless the brand has genuine scan data velocity, pass.
  • Cognac without trade conviction: Recovery depends on simultaneous U.S. and China resolution. Rémy Cointreau’s XO exposure (−26.4% volume) is acute. Only for investors with specific trade-policy insight.
  • Undifferentiated craft spirits: 89% of craft producers share 11% of revenue. Acquiring a brand without secured distribution is acquiring a brand without a business.
  • Value vodka: Super-premium vodka down 15% in 2024. The segment is in permanent structural decline from RTD substitution, health trends, and tequila migration.
